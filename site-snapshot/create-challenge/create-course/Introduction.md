### Section 3 — What Is Assistive Technology? (white)
Label: "Disability & Assistive Technology Primer"

Definition from Assistive Technology Act of 1998: "any item, piece of equipment, or product system...used to increase, maintain, or improve functional capabilities of individuals with disabilities."

Explanation that AT is defined by its *use* by a person with a disability (contrast with other technology categories). Examples: closed captioning, home assistants.

Categories list: mobility/seating/positioning; environmental control; sensory deficits; communication; computer inputs/outputs; cognitive limitations.

**Subheading: Multiple Views**
Resource links:
- PACER Center: "Understanding Assistive Technology: Simply Said" — https://youtu.be/DB9pKkZoJDc
- MassAbility definition — https://www.mass.gov/info-details/what-is-assistive-technology

### Section 4 — People with Disabilities as Co-Designers (gray)
Label: "Co-Design Philosophy"

Intro on working *with* co-designers, not just *for* people with disabilities. Broader view: co-designers may have difficulty with daily activities beyond traditionally defined disabilities.

**Subheading: "Clients" and "Co-Designers"**
Rationale for "co-designer" term; ensures products actually solve the challenge and don't create new barriers. Co-designers may fill team roles (leader, PM, designer, etc.).

**Subheading: Product Specifications**
Product specs explicitly include accessibility needs; product must help overcome barriers without creating new ones.

**Reflect callout box**
"Assistive technology and disability are dynamic concepts: they take on different shades of meaning depending on a specific person's context."
