# Additional Resources

## CAD / 3D Modeling

- **[Onshape](https://www.onshape.com/)** — Cloud-based CAD program with collaboration capabilities. Used as BWSI AT's baseline CAD software since it is platform-agnostic; free academic license.
- **[Fusion 360](https://www.autodesk.com/products/fusion-360/)** — CAD program by Autodesk with design, engineering, electronics, and manufacturing capabilities; free with academic license.
- **[Tinkercad](https://www.tinkercad.com)** — Beginner-friendly browser-based 3D modeling tool; completely free and great for students new to CAD.
- **[FreeCAD](https://www.freecad.org/)** — Open-source parametric 3D modeling software for mechanical engineering and product design.
- **[Blender](https://www.blender.org/)** — Free and open-source 3D creation suite for modeling, rendering, and animation.
- **[Keyshot 3D](https://www.keyshot.com/)** — Professional rendering and animation software for creating photorealistic 3D visuals and product animations.

## Electronics & Programming

- **[Arduino](https://www.arduino.cc/)** — Open-source electronics platform with free IDE; widely used for prototyping interactive devices.
- **[Raspberry Pi](https://www.raspberrypi.com/)** — Low-cost single-board computer; excellent for programming and electronics projects.
- **[MIT App Inventor](https://appinventor.mit.edu/)** — Free, browser-based tool developed at MIT for building mobile apps without coding experience.
- **[Scratch](https://scratch.mit.edu/)** — Free visual programming language developed by MIT Media Lab; great for learning programming concepts.

### Circuit Design

- **[Tinkercad Circuits](https://www.tinkercad.com/circuits)** — Free browser-based circuit simulator integrated with Tinkercad.
- **[Fritzing](https://fritzing.org/)** — Open-source electronics design automation software for prototyping circuits.
- **[KiCad](https://www.kicad.org/)** — Free and open-source electronics design automation suite for PCB design.

### Documentation & Collaboration

- **[Inkscape](https://inkscape.org/)** — Free and open-source vector graphics editor for creating diagrams and technical illustrations.
- **[GIMP](https://www.gimp.org/)** — Free and open-source image editor.
- **[OBS Studio](https://obsproject.com/)** — Free and open-source software for video recording and live streaming (useful for documenting projects).

### Simulation & Testing

- **[OpenSCAD](https://openscad.org/)** — Free script-based 3D CAD modeler; particularly useful for parametric designs.

## MIT Course 6.811: Principles and Practice of Assistive Technology

- **[Fall 2014 on OpenCourseWare](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/electrical-engineering-and-computer-science/6-811-principles-and-practice-of-assistive-technology-fall-2014/)** — Free online course materials including lectures, assignments, and project examples from MIT's foundational AT course.
- **[Fall 2019 class page](http://ppat.mit.edu/fall2019/index.html)** — Course website with syllabus, schedule, and additional resources.

## MIT Assistive Technology Club

- **[AT Club](https://edgerton.mit.edu/assistive-technology-club)** — Student organization focused on assistive technology design, development, and community engagement at MIT.

## D4AT

Alumni of the 2021 summer course started a nationwide set of assistive technology clubs called D4AT in the 2021–2022 academic year, with four active chapters as well as support from additional schools.

- **[The D4AT curriculum](https://student-at-program.github.io/onlinecourse/)** — A public fork of the BWSI AT pre-course.
- **[D4AT Virtual Maker Faire](https://designforat.org/)** — A virtual showcase featuring 10 different student project presentations on their co-design work with people in their communities with disabilities.
