# Electronics

Electronics are important for making devices that can **sense the world** and **respond dynamically**. A basic understanding of the underlying physics, circuit design, and coding is helpful.

Free simulators are available to get started before investing in hardware.

---

## Electrical Engineering Principles

Before tackling electronics projects, review:
- **[Introduction to Electrical Engineering](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/electrical-engineering/introduction-to-ee/intro-to-ee)** (Khan Academy)
- **[Circuit Analysis](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/electrical-engineering/ee-circuit-analysis-topic)** (Khan Academy)

These two units cover current, voltage, and power — the fundamentals needed for most project work.

---

## Circuits and Microcontrollers

Many electronics projects begin with a **microcontroller board** — integrated circuit hardware with software support for controlling behavior.

### Popular platforms:

- **Arduino Uno** — common starter board; widely supported
- **Adafruit Feather** — smaller form factor, particularly suited for wearable applications
- **Raspberry Pi** — technically a full Linux computer, but its GPIO (General Purpose Input/Output) pins allow similar electronics interfacing to the other boards; far more powerful

Starter kits exist for each ecosystem; many basic components are interchangeable across them.

### Simulator first:

**[Tinkercad Circuits](https://www.tinkercad.com/learn/circuits)** — free browser-based electronics simulator. Recommended workflow:
1. Complete introductory sections (Start Simulating → Series and Parallel Circuits)
2. Complete Arduino-specific sections and guided projects most relevant to your project
3. Free account required
