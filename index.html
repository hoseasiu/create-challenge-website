The CRE[AT]E Assistive Technology Challenge

Design assistive technology.
Make a real difference.

The CRE[AT]E Challenge is a design and engineering program for high school and college students across the United States.

Learn about disability and product design while working in teams to prototype a solution for someone in your community with disability-related challenges. No prior experience required — just curiosity and a willingness to listen and build.

📚
Online Course
Based on MIT's 6.811 Principles and Practice of Assistive Technology. Learn at your own pace.
🤝
Community of Learners
Connect with peers across the country to share ideas and refine your designs.
🔬
Expert Design Reviews
Get feedback from technical mentors with expertise in AT and engineering.
💡
Funding for Your Project
Receive funding to prototype and build your assistive technology solution.

Part of MIT Beaver Works

Beaver Works Assistive Technology is offered through the MIT Lincoln Laboratory Beaver Works Summer Institute, which teaches engineering design to students through hands-on projects that improve people's lives. We currently run the CRE[AT]E Challenge; our previous programs include Design of AT and Design for Education Aids.
