CRE[AT]E Course

A self-paced curriculum for building assistive technology

The CRE[AT]E Course is the educational foundation of the challenge. It teaches student teams how to understand disability and assistive technology, run a co-design process, develop and sketch ideas, build hands-on skills, and document their work for the Final Event.

Teams work through the course alongside their project, applying each module's concepts directly to their co-designer's needs. Registered team coaches also receive classroom activity guides for teaching the material in person.

How it works

Five modules, completed at your own pace

The course is organized into five modules. Teams should work through them roughly in order, though the Maker Skills module can be tackled alongside other modules once a project direction is established.

📖
Self-paced
Each module is available online at any time. Teams move at their own speed and revisit sections as their project evolves.
🤝
Co-design focused
Every module connects back to the co-designer relationship — the course teaches skills in the context of real, human-centered problem solving.
🔧
Hands-on skills
Maker Skills covers the main technical areas used in CRE[AT]E projects — teams choose the two sections most relevant to their build.
🏫
Classroom guides
Registered coaches receive activity guides for teaching the course material to their team in a classroom setting.

Course modules

Work through the course in five parts

Each module builds on the last. Start with Introduction to ground your team in disability and co-design, then move through the design process, idea development, making, and documentation.

Module 1

Introduction

Understand disability from multiple perspectives, learn what assistive technology is and how it's defined, and explore the co-design philosophy that guides the whole challenge. Includes video profiles, real-world AT examples, and reflection activities.

Go to Introduction →

Module 2

Design Processes

Learn the engineering design loop used in CRE[AT]E projects: understand, brainstorm, prototype, test, and iterate. This module introduces human-centered design and the specific iterative approach teams use throughout their project.

Go to Design Processes →

Module 3

Idea to Ink

Translate ideas into documented designs. Learn sketching techniques for product concepts, storyboarding usage scenarios, creating technical drawings, and writing design specifications that communicate intent clearly to your team and co-designer.

Go to Idea to Ink →

Module 4

Maker Skills

Build the hands-on skills your project needs. This module has four sections — Mechanical Making, Software, Electronics, and Wearables. Teams are asked to complete the two sections most relevant to their project. Additional resources are available on Discord.

Go to Maker Skills →

Module 5

Documentation and Presentation

Learn how to record your design decisions, build process, and test results, and how to present your work effectively. Covers written reports, video demonstrations, and slide presentations — the formats used at the Final Event.

Go to Documentation and Presentation →

Maker Skills

Choose the two sections most relevant to your project

Assistive technology projects use a wide range of skills depending on what's being built. The Maker Skills module is divided into four areas — complete the two that best match your team's project direction. If your project uses skills not covered in any section, reach out on Discord for additional guidance.

Mechanical Making
Physical fabrication techniques and mechanical engineering fundamentals for designing and building AT devices that move, hold, support, or interact with the physical world.

Mechanical Making →
Software
Programming concepts and development techniques for building software components of AT solutions — from microcontroller code to accessible interfaces.

Software →
Electronics
Circuit design, component selection, and electronics integration for AT projects — from basic circuits to sensors, actuators, and control systems.

Electronics →
Wearables
Techniques for designing technology worn on the body — including materials, form, comfort, and integrating electronics into garments or wearable structures.

Wearables →
