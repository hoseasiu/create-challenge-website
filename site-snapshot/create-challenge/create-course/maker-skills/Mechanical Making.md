# Mechanical Making

## Machine Tools and Shops

A machine shop is a workshop with tools primarily used for **subtractive manufacturing** — the making of products by cutting into materials — such as lathes, mills, drill presses, and laser cutters. Most machine shops now also include tools for **additive manufacturing**, such as 3D printers.

The page includes an embedded YouTube video: [StuffMadeHere shop tour](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SjJulcvTA7Y) to give students a sense of what tools are commonly available.

A virtual tour of the **MIT Beaver Works space** is available at [my.matterport.com](https://my.matterport.com/show/?m=rJtbANQgip3) — students are encouraged to find the prototyping shop and identify tools and safety equipment.

For the CRE[AT]E Challenge, teams should also consider what maker tools are available at home, through school, prototyping services, or a local makerspace. The module asks students to find and describe a makerspace near them (name, location, available tools, access process).

## 3D Printing

3D printing is a collection of methods by which a computer-controlled device creates a three-dimensional object by adding parts bit by bit. Used for rapid prototyping of products.

An introductory YouTube video on 3D printing is embedded on the page.

### Comprehension questions students are asked:

1. What is the most common type of 3D printing filament?
2. What is G-code, and how is it usually generated?
3. What does infill refer to?
4. What are the ways that model orientation might affect a 3D print?
5. For an FDM printer, what is another reason to use certain model orientations over others? *(Hint: lever printed lying horizontal vs. standing vertical — layering affects strength)*

## Computer-Aided Design (CAD)

Introduces **Onshape** — a web-based CAD program used as BWSI AT's baseline CAD software because:
- Free for academic use
- Platform-agnostic (runs in browser)
- Supports collaboration

### Getting started with Onshape:
1. Create a free account at [onshape.com/products/education](https://www.onshape.com/products/education)
2. Complete these Self-Paced Courses in the [Onshape Learning Center](https://learn.onshape.com):
   - Navigating Onshape
   - Introduction to Sketching
   - Part Design Using Part Studios

Other CAD options mentioned: **Fusion 360** and **Tinkercad** (both by Autodesk, free for students). See also [[Additional Resources]].
