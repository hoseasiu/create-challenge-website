# Tips for Teams

## How can I maximize my chances of having a good time and learning from the Challenge?

The CRE[AT]E Challenge has two parts: a [course](https://beaver-works-assistive-tech.mit.edu/create-challenge/create-course) which supports a [project](https://beaver-works-assistive-tech.mit.edu/create-challenge/create-project). Successful teams should plan to meet approximately weekly for most of the season to be able to complete these parts, and likely more often (including possibly weekends and evenings) in preparation for design reviews, for project work, and to meet with co-designers.

Remember that co-designers have their own lives and schedules you will need to work around — don't expect your partners to bend to your last-minute testing requests. Familiarize yourself with the [schedule](https://beaver-works-assistive-tech.mit.edu/create-challenge/schedule) for key milestones and plan accordingly. Also remember that you will need to coordinate peer reviews with other teams, which may not be in the same time zone as you. Include your time zone when coordinating, or agree on a single time zone (Beaver Works uses Eastern Time for live events and Anywhere on Earth for assignments).

### What works well

- **Manage your time.** Plan for weekly team work, live events, and co-designer meetings. Build a schedule around milestone dates so you’re not rushed at the end.
- **Brainstorm.** Don’t get stuck on a single idea at the beginning. Generate many possible solutions and evaluate several ways to solve your co-designer’s stated problem.
- **Solve the problem.** Focus on solving your co-designer’s real need rather than building the coolest-looking product. Simple solutions can be elegant and often work best.
- **Get help, give help.** Ask teammates, staff, mentors, and other teams for support. Use the Discord server to ask questions, and if you can, help another team too — the community is part of the Challenge experience.
- **Learn to work as a team.** Identify each team member’s strengths and assign roles accordingly. Treat your co-designer as part of the team and invite them into each design iteration.

## How can I maximize my chances of not having a good time?

Bad ideas that we have seen teams enthusiastically execute.

1. **Don't communicate.** Communication keeps teammates, mentors, staff, and your co-designer informed. Isolating your team during a community-focused challenge is the quickest way to increase frustration and risk.
2. **Treat everything as a competition.** The Challenge is not fundamentally a contest. Focus on learning, co-design, and solving a real problem rather than trying to outperform other teams.
3. **Come up with a product first and then find a co-designer.** If you build for your own idea instead of a co-designer’s real need, your project is unlikely to be feasible or meaningful.
4. **Ignore the course.** The course is part of the Challenge and supports your project work. Skipping it usually makes the work harder, limits your learning, and reduces your chances of success.
