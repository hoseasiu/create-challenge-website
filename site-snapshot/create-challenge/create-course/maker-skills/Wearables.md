# Wearables

Wearables are technology **worn on the body** — from smart watches and body-mounted sensors, to electronics embedded in clothing, to purely mechanical devices like arm and leg braces. Many CRE[AT]E Challenge projects take the form of a wearable for the co-designer.

Electronic wearables typically involve: microprocessors, sensors, a power source (usually lithium-ion), and output devices (LEDs, screens, motors, haptic feedback, or data analytics).

---

## Electronic Wearables — Components

- **Microprocessors** — Arduino is popular for prototyping, but **Adafruit's Lilypad, Bluefruit, and Feather** boards are specifically designed with wearables in mind (smaller form factor, power-conscious)
- **Sensors** — collect data from the user's body or environment
- **Power Source** — most wearables use small rechargeable lithium-ion batteries; energy efficiency is important
- **Output Devices** — LED lights, touchscreen displays, motors, haptic feedback, or data sent for analytics

---

## Key Considerations for Wearables

### 1. Form Factor & Ergonomics

- **Minimizing discomfort** — ergonomics is critical; think about pressure points, irritation, and strain during use
- **Donning and doffing** — can the user put it on/take it off independently? Are the movements required appropriate for the user? Simplify where possible

### 2. Measuring for Wearables

Bodies are complex and dynamic:

- **Bodies are flexible** — measure multiple times; look up anatomical measurement procedures for consistency
- **Bodies move** — does the wearable placement flex or shift? Consider elastic or knit fabrics to prevent slipping
- **Bodies change** — children grow; people in rehabilitation change. Add adjustability for long-term usability
- **Bodies need hygiene** — wearables in extended skin contact must be cleanable. Consider washable covers or sweat/odor-resistant materials

### 3. Aesthetics

Wearable products are **more visible** in the user's daily life than most other AT products. How the product looks can directly affect whether it gets used — especially for children. Consider the co-designer's aesthetic opinions and adjust accordingly.

## Wearable Technology Curriculum

The CRE[AT]E Challenge partners with the **[Advanced Functional Fabrics of America (AFFOA)](https://affoa.org/)** — one of 17 Manufacturing USA institutes — whose mission is to rekindle the domestic textiles industry through advanced fiber and fabric technology.
