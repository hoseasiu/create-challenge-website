Getting started

What new CRE[AT]E Challenge teams need first

This page helps new student teams begin the CRE[AT]E Challenge by explaining the registration process, team roles, co-designer partnerships, and the first steps through the CRE[AT]E Course materials.

Use this guide to prepare before you register.

How to start

Four key steps

  1. Understand the challenge. CRE[AT]E teams build assistive technology with a co-designer who has a disability or daily-living challenge.
  2. Form your student team. Most teams include 2–5 students with a range of skills, from design and electronics to software and documentation.
  3. Find a co-designer. Choose someone you can work with closely as a partner, ideally someone who wants a solution for a real need.
  4. Review the course materials. The CRE[AT]E Course is how teams learn design methods, maker skills, and project documentation step by step.

Register

Sign up your team and submit your registration form

Registration is the first formal step. You will provide team details, a brief description of your co-designer relationship, and contact information so the Beaver Works staff can connect you with mentors and the challenge timeline.

Build your team

Make a strong student team and co-designer partnership

Successful teams balance curiosity, communication, and a commitment to the co-designer’s goals. The strongest teams stay organized and keep the co-designer involved from the beginning.

Team size
Most teams are 2–5 students. Smaller teams can move fast, while larger teams can support more roles — just make sure everyone has clear responsibilities.
Co-designer role
Your co-designer is a partner, not just a user. They should help define the problem, test ideas, and give feedback during the whole project.
  • Choose students with different strengths. Design, fabrication, coding, research, and presentation skills all help.
  • Include a communicator. One student should take primary responsibility for meeting planning, follow-up, and co-designer coordination.
  • Keep meetings regular. Weekly check-ins help the team stay on track and keep the co-designer involved.

Find a co-designer

Choose someone with a meaningful challenge to solve

A co-designer can be a family member, friend, neighbor, teacher, or community member with a disability or a daily-living challenge. The right match is someone who wants to work with your team and is excited to shape the solution.

  1. Start close to home. Ask people you already know first, then reach out to local disability services, special education staff, or community organizations.
  2. Focus on real needs. A good project begins with a problem the co-designer faces every day, not a hypothetical idea.
  3. Respect consent and privacy. Talk through what information will be shared and how your team will keep the co-designer comfortable.
Need outreach ideas?
Talk with teachers, club advisors, school nurses, therapists, or local disability groups. They often know people who want to participate in student design projects.
What to ask first
Ask about daily routines, tasks that feel difficult, and what the co-designer would most like to change. That helps your team focus on a real co-designer goal.

Begin the course

Start learning the CRE[AT]E Course materials

The CRE[AT]E Course teaches teams how to turn a co-designer need into a usable assistive technology prototype. Begin with the introduction modules and move through idea development, maker skills, and documentation.

  • Introduction. Learn the project flow, the team milestones, and how co-design works.
  • Idea to Ink. Capture early ideas, sketch concepts, and narrow your project scope.
  • Maker Skills. Build skills in electronics, software, wearables, and mechanical making.
  • Documentation and Presentation. Practice recording your design process and preparing your final challenge submission.

If your team is new to assistive technology, spend extra time on co-designer interviews and needs analysis before committing to a final concept.

Next steps

Get ready to launch your project

Once your team is registered and your co-designer is on board, begin the challenge by setting a meeting schedule, gathering your tools, and reviewing the first course module.

You should also bookmark the current forms page and keep an eye out for email updates from Beaver Works staff about milestone deadlines, mentor sessions, and submission guidelines.

Keep documentation early
Take notes in every co-designer meeting and save photos of sketches, prototypes, and test setups. Good documentation makes the final report much easier.
Ask for help
Reach out to challenge staff and technical mentors as soon as you have questions — they are there to support your first prototype and your team’s learning.

Ready to begin the CRE[AT]E Challenge?

Register your team, confirm your co-designer partnership, and start working through the course materials early so you can focus on building something meaningful.
